# Behavioral Programs and Safety Boundaries

BIVE analyzes transcripts and evidence structures. AOS governs automation behavior. The combined system must not become an accusation engine, lie detector, diagnostic engine, or unrestricted autonomous actor.

## Allowed behavioral programs

- Evidence extraction.
- Contradiction surfacing.
- Missing-evidence questions.
- Human-review task generation.
- Automation risk classification.
- Execution contract generation.
- Verification gate generation.
- Regression and repair planning.

## Forbidden behavioral programs

- Automatic person-level liar label.
- Guilt verdict.
- Medical or psychological diagnosis.
- Intent attribution without evidence.
- Public action without review.
- Secret exposure.
- Irreversible automation without rollback and human gate.

## Engineering rule

Behavioral inference must collapse into evidence, uncertainty, or review questions. If it cannot be verified, it must not become a decision.
