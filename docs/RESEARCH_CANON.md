# Research Canon

## Core position

Deception detection must be treated as uncertain multimodal hypothesis review. Nonverbal cues are weak and frequently overbelieved. Verbal cues, contextual contradiction, and evidence provenance are generally more defensible than theatrical gesture reading.

## Canonical claims for this repository

1. There is no universal behavioral cue that proves lying.
2. Human unaided deception detection is usually weak, often near chance in broad settings.
3. Verbal content and contextual consistency tend to be more useful than isolated nonverbal behavior.
4. Micro-expressions and action units can guide attention but cannot decide truth.
5. Polygraph-like physiological arousal is not truth.
6. Multimodal fusion must be calibrated and validated per dataset/domain.
7. Reports must include counter-evidence and uncertainty.

## Research directions

- Verbal deception cues and cognitive load.
- Multimodal fusion under distribution shift.
- Action Unit extraction and facial behavior analysis.
- Speaker diarization and transcript alignment.
- Provenance and reproducibility for ML pipelines.
- Adversarial robustness against acting, editing, deepfakes, compression, and context removal.
